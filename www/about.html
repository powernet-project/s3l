    Professor Ram Rajagopal is a joint faculty member of Civil and Environmental Engineering (CEE), as well Electrical Engineering (EE) at Stanford University

    Raffi Sevlian -

    Haeyoung Noh is a postdoctoral scholar working with Professor Anne Kiremidjian and Professor Ram Rajagopal. Her research concerns the analysis and control of dynamic systems using statistical signal processing and machine learning techniques, particularly algorithm development for smart structures and systems to conserve energy and resources and provide safe, functional, and sustainable environments. 

    Adrian Albert is a PhD student in Electrical Engineering. His work investigates demand-side processes that take place in infrastructure systems (energy, transportation), where the human influence is substantial. He works on analytics for smart grid data, on analyzing the impact of energy policy on freight transportation demand, and on modeling shock processes on infrastructure networks.

    Jeffrey Wong is a MS student in Statistics. His work is on big data computing, statistical learning, load forecasting, and time series clustering.

    Junjie Qin is a graduate student in ICME affiliated with Stanford Sustainable Systems Lab. He is currently investigating stochastic optimal control of power systems in the framework of risk limiting dispatch.

    Mark Mollineaux -

    Sean Qian's research strives to analyze dynamic transportation/infrastructure networks through theories of network flow and economics and by applying advanced optimization and statistical tools. The various components of this research endeavor have wide applications in transportation engineering, infrastructure engineering, planning and system operations. Sean is currently working on dynamic parking management. In addition to real-time traffic data on freeways and arterials, dynamic parking occupancy and pricing data are collected, which are then used to set up optimum dynamic pricing schemesfor garages and on-street parking. Novel parking network models incorporating stochastic parking choices are proposed. We optimize the parking dynamic pricing, parking information provision and space allocations toimprove the system efficiency and mitigate the congestion.
